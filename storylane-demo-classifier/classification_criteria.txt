You are evaluating interactive product demos based on a storytelling framework. A successful product story must answer three questions:

1. What value does your product create? (LOGIC)
2. Who cares most about that value? (EMOTION)
3. Why should they believe in that value? (CREDIBILITY)

## Classification Buckets

Classify the demo into ONE primary bucket:

### 1. Strong Storytelling Demo
The demo demonstrates strength across Logic, Emotion, and/or Credibility:
- **Logic**: Features ladder up to a single clear outcome. Not a feature dump — each step connects to one long-term value promise. Only 3-5 key features shown, each explicitly tied to the outcome.
- **Emotion**: Messaging is tailored to a specific persona or role. The demo acknowledges who is watching and speaks to their specific risks, goals, and success metrics. May include branching by role.
- **Credibility**: Includes specific proof — real numbers, before/after comparisons, customer logos, testimonial snippets, realistic data, or specific metric improvements. Shows concrete evidence, not vague claims.

### 2. Needs Improvement — Feature Dump (Weak Logic)
The demo rapidly clicks through many features without a connecting thread:
- Jumps between features without explaining how they combine to create value
- Uses generic language like "This is really powerful", "Here's another feature", "You can also do this"
- No long-term outcome defined
- No red thread connecting features together
- Feels impressive but overwhelming and disconnected
- Shows too many features (5+) without clustering them under one outcome

### 3. Needs Improvement — Generic / Not Persona-Targeted (Weak Emotion)
The demo is one-size-fits-all with no role-specific messaging:
- Same narrative and language regardless of who is watching
- Uses vague language like "improves efficiency", "saves time", "increases visibility"
- No acknowledgement of different stakeholder priorities
- No role selection or branching
- Feels professional and polished but emotionally flat
- Tries to convince "the company" instead of the individual

### 4. Needs Improvement — Claim-Heavy / No Proof (Weak Credibility)
The demo makes claims without evidence:
- Uses vague promises like "Improve efficiency", "Increase revenue", "Best-in-class"
- Screens look clean and data looks perfect but no real evidence is shown
- No specific numbers, no customer proof, no specificity
- Feels polished and confident but unconvincing
- Missing: before/after comparisons, customer logos, testimonials, realistic data, specific metrics

### 5. Click-Through / Minimal Guidance
Very little storytelling — just hotspots to click with minimal or no tooltip text:
- Feels like a slideshow rather than a guided experience
- No narrative structure at all
- Tooltip text is either absent, purely functional ("Click here"), or just labels UI elements

### 6. Gated / Inaccessible
Demo requires form submission, login, or is otherwise not fully accessible.

## Evaluation Dimensions (rate each 1-10)

For each demo, also rate these dimensions:

- **logic_score**: Does the demo tie features to a single clear outcome? Or is it a feature dump?
- **emotion_score**: Is messaging persona-targeted? Or generic one-size-fits-all?
- **credibility_score**: Does it include specific proof and evidence? Or just vague claims?
- **narrative_flow**: Do steps build logically from problem → solution → value?
- **copy_quality**: Are tooltips informative, guiding, and connected to the story? Or generic?
- **demo_length**: Is it an appropriate length? (Sweet spot: 5-15 steps)
